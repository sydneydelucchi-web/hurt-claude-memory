## Design Weight Rules (for skill auto-decisions)

### Platform-Specific Design Preferences
**LinkedIn (primary growth engine — 66.0% of all interactions, +105 followers this period):**
- Optimize for: conference/event recaps with forward-looking close, data-backed thought leadership, product launches
- Visual style: Bold stat callouts, dark backgrounds with red accents, professional photography
- Best posting window: 2–6 PM EST (per Rella AI insight)
- Format priority: Single image with strong headline overlay > carousel
- Design weight: **55% of design effort**
- Key insight: "Partners get the full picture first" exclusive close formula — replicate on every major event post. Long-form posts (150+ words) with forward-looking narrative drive highest interactions. New engagement record: 19.5%.

**Instagram (engagement engine — 23.2% engagement rate, highest per-platform ↑):**
- Optimize for: visual storytelling, partner-shareable content, carousels, reels
- Visual style: Bright brand colors, carousel slides with clear progression, partner co-brand ready
- Format priority: Carousel (confirmed #1) > Reel > Single image
- Design weight: **25% of design effort**
- Key insight: Real photography outperforms designed graphics. Reach recovering (+13.8% this period) — watch for sustained improvement. Reactive sports carousels are the engagement engine.
- Opportunity: Reactive sports carousel content is +13% engagement — prioritize for every sports injury news cycle.

**Facebook (reach amplifier — 12.0% interaction share, reach +41.7% this period):**
- Design weight: **10%** — do NOT raw cross-post from IG/LinkedIn
- Create FB-native versions: shorter copy, utility-focused, stat-led, question-driven CTAs
- Facebook is now the #1 reach channel by raw numbers (4,594) — treat as awareness/distribution
- Baseline engagement ceiling: ~2.7% — don't optimize for engagement, optimize for reach and click-through
- Growth accelerating: +7 followers vs +2 last period — something is driving new fans

**TikTok (minimal activity — 700 followers, 99 views this period):**
- Design weight: 5% — create IG Reel-compatible vertical video assets for cross-post
- Note: Views suggest some content posted but zero interactions — content-type mismatch likely
- Opportunity: March Madness reactive sports content is the proven formula — test it here

**YouTube (nascent — 11 followers, 100 views):**
- Design weight: 5% — repurpose long-form content, no bespoke design needed yet

### Content Type Design Rules
1. **Conference/Event content (NEW #1 theme)**: Use real photography, "what's next" forward-looking narrative with exclusive partner close ("Partners get the full picture first. If you're in the network, watch your inbox."). Dark or brand-red backgrounds. Design once for LinkedIn dimensions, adapt crop for IG. This formula produced a 19.5% engagement rate — replicate on every conference.
2. **Reactive sports content**: Name specific athletes/events, frame the system gap, connect to HURT! product (ATC, HDR). Dark backgrounds, bold stats, IG Carousel format. Every major sports injury news cycle gets a carousel.
3. **Product launch (HDR/new features)**: Teal color system for HDR, carousel format for IG, bold stat-forward single image for LinkedIn. Still top reach/views driver.
4. **Challenger/POV content**: Dark backgrounds, bold Urbanist headlines. Use specific-number hooks, not vague provocation. Works on IG but NOT Facebook — never cross-post without native adaptation.
5. **Partner announcements**: Clean white/light backgrounds, partner logo prominent, HURT! logo secondary. Designed to be reshared by partner practices.
6. **Patient journey/narrative**: Photography-forward, warm tones, minimal branded overlay. LinkedIn-first.

### Shareability Design Principles (Sydney's #1 metric: shares)
- **Story-reshare ready**: Every IG post should look intentional when screenshotted into a story
- **Partner co-brand clean**: Logo placement that allows partner overlay without visual collision
- **Bold headline visible at thumbnail size**: If the hook isn't readable at 160×160px, redesign
- **Challenger POV = scroll-stop**: Use tension in the headline, not in the design — design stays clean, copy does the provoking
- **Dark backgrounds outperform on LinkedIn**: Top LinkedIn post used dark/rich visual treatment
- **Insider tease close**: "Partners get the full picture first" as a closing line is now proven to drive saves/shares — build it into conference content templates

### Seasonal Design Calendar
- **Spring (Mar–May)**: Spring sports injuries (baseball, soccer, lacrosse, track), March Madness basketball injuries
- **Summer (Jun–Aug)**: Summer sports (swimming, cycling, running), outdoor activity injuries, vacation injuries
- **Fall (Sep–Nov)**: Football season (NFL + college), back-to-school sports physicals, fall running season
- **Winter (Dec–Feb)**: Ski/snowboard injuries, winter slip-and-fall, New Year fitness injuries, Super Bowl
- **Year-round orthopedic awareness**: National Arthritis Month (May), Bone & Joint Health Week (Oct), PT Month (Oct)
- **Conference season**: AAOS (Mar), OrthoForum (Mar), various regional orthopedic meetings — highest-ROI content window of the year
