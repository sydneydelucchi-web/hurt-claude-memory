Social media content should follow these guidelines:
- **Tone:** Confident, warm, expert but accessible. Never clinical jargon without patient-friendly translation.
- **Platform strategy:** Instagram for patient-facing education and brand; LinkedIn for employer/B2B and thought leadership; Facebook for community engagement and partner amplification.
- **Caption style:** Short, punchy lead line. Break into scannable sections. End with CTA. Use line breaks liberally.
- **Content mix priorities:** Patient education (stretches, tips, injury prevention) gets highest engagement. Employer MSK cost content for LinkedIn. After-hours access narrative is a key differentiator. Partner success stories build credibility.
- **Visual direction:** Clean, warm, on-brand. HURT! red and teal. No stock photo feel. Prefer Canva templates for consistency.
- **Hashtag approach:** Branded (#HURTApp #JointScore #DigitalRehab) + category (#SportsMediacine #OrthopedicCare #PhysicalTherapy) + local (#BayArea #AustinTX etc based on geo).
- **Scheduling:** Recommends content blocks above trending data. Quick-schedule buttons on all content ideas.
- **Carousel/Reel preference:** Carousel for educational content, Reels for engagement hooks, Stories for behind-the-scenes.
- **UX preference:** Simplified naming (Posts, Calendar, Trends, News vs longer names). Clean sidebar. Feature-based content tagging by HURT! product line.

**Why:** Sydney is building a dashboard for partner marketing teams who may not be marketing-savvy. Everything needs to be intuitive, on-brand, and actionable with minimal steps.

**How to apply:** All AI-generated captions, recommendations, and content suggestions should follow this voice. Tailor by platform. Include geo-relevant hooks when location data is available.
